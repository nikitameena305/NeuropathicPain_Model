# Project 5 Easy Explanation

## Big picture

In neuropathic pain, the spinal dorsal horn can become overactive.  
One major reason is loss of inhibition.

GABA normally works like a brake.  
When this brake becomes weak, excitatory receptors such as AMPA and NMDA can make projection neurons fire more.

Projection neurons are important because they send pain signals upward to the brain.

## Core idea

AMPA and NMDA are both glutamate receptors, but they work differently.

AMPA is fast.  
It gives quick firing during stimulus.

NMDA is slow.  
It can keep firing going even after the stimulus has stopped.

So in this project, we ask:

After GABA loss, is NMDA responsible for persistent firing?

## Design

Four conditions were tested:

1. Normal AMPA/NMDA  
2. Reduced GABA  
3. Reduced GABA + NMDA block  
4. Reduced GABA + AMPA block  

## Expected pattern

Reduced GABA should increase projection-neuron firing.

NMDA block should reduce late firing.

AMPA block should reduce fast stimulus-driven firing.

## Simple analogy

GABA = brake  
AMPA = quick spark  
NMDA = slow-burning coal  
Projection neuron = pain-output neuron  

When brake is weak, slow-burning NMDA can keep the system active for longer.

## Main conclusion to check

If late firing decreases after NMDA block, then NMDA contributes to persistent pain-like output after GABA loss.
